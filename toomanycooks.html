Developing Too Many Cooks

// Game design, UI/UX design, animation, Unity C#

March - May 2019

Summary

Too Many Cooks is an “Overcooked”-style video game with an apocalyptic twist. This
single player game challenges the user’s speed and time management skills all while
combating multiple enemies.

I was in charge of creating all art assets, including character and environment sprites,
animations, and UI design.

My Role

  • Concept Design
  • Art
  • Animation

    • Obstacles

    My role was originally a hybrid of artist and programmer. However, the lack of artists
    in the class meant that I had to adjust my role to full-time artist. Being the sole artist
    to 3 other programmers, it was difficult to keep up with the team’s pace. Spending
    overtime hours to finish the requirements for each week took a toll on the quality of
    my work. I had many ideas for adding more styles and costumes to the character, but
    time restricted my ability to execute these ideas. When told the group about my
    difficulties in handling the workload, they were willing to adjust the tight schedule.

    Learnings

    I learned the importance of limiting scope when working on a big project. The
    development process can take much longer than what was originally planned, even
    with strict deadlines and structured schedules. Though adding a lot of features can
    be exciting, I learned that it is more meaningful to hone the core game mechanic.
    Focusing on the core good mechanic instead of glittering the game is always a better
    use of time.
