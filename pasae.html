Marketing PASAE

// Apparels, banners, flyers, business card

August 2016 - May 2019

Summary

PASAE is an on-campus organization at UC Berkeley aimed towards providing
academic, professional, and social support to Filipino American students in STE(A)M.
Activities include career workshops, panel discussions, networking, and more.

I lead the team that held all media relations, including but not limited to online
content and promotional merchandise. I also designed event banners, flyers, business
cards, apparel, stickers, and more.

My Role

  • Creative Direction
  • Graphic Design
  • Marketing

    • Cover Designs

    For each event, PASAE needed Facebook cover designs targeted towards club
    members and other students. The main idea was to capture attention as people
    scroll through their feeds. Additionally, it is important to share the general
    information in the image so that people can quickly decide if they are interested and
    click on the event link. One concern is that Facebook adjusts the image in different
    sizes depending on the device, so I made sure that the text is always in the center.
    This way, the most important information will always be seen.

    Team Wear

    It is important to create a sense of unity and belonging to build close-knit
    relationships. For that reason, I carefully designed team wear each semester, taking
    into account what type and color of clothing the members liked.

    Business Cards

    Since PASAE has a lot of networking events year-round, business cards allow
    members to share contact information with professionals for potential collaborations.
    The first official PASAE business cards were made using our logo colors.

    Obstacles

    Not too long ago, PASAE’s website suffered with little views and activity. At one point,
    the previous webmaster stopped updating it with new content. As lead of the
    creative team, I had to find ways to recover the dying website. I tried to communicate
    with the webmaster about the need for newer content, but there was still no
    progress. The hardest part was needing to let go of a staff member. I and the other
    execs handled the situation carefully and made sure both sides parted agreeably.
    After finding a new webmaster, our team thought of ways we can make the website
    more interactive and useful. With these ideas executed by the new webmaster,
    we increased the site’s views significantly.

    Learnings

    Being part of PASAE for 3 years, I learned the key aspects of managing an
    organization from the inside out. Taking on a leadership role has taught me to voice
    my thoughts and ideas more confidently. This also means working out the differences
    of each member and fostering an environment where everyone feels valuable.
