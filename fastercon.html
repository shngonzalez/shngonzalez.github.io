Branding FASTERCon

// Logo design, brochure, banners

June 2017 - present

Summary

FASTERCon is an annual networking conference for Filipino Americans in STE(A)M,
co-hosted by UC Berkeley’s student-run organizations and FASTER Professionals. The
conference gathers over 200 attendees each year, from top tech companies to local
startups.

I worked on various designs for marketing the event including a virtual character, a
12-page print program, banners, and giveaways.

My Role

  • Branding
  • Graphic Design

    • Concept Idea

    When I first started working with FASTER in 2017, the network highly centered around
    Silicon Valley tech. The goal was to create a hi-tech feel through electronic-style
    graphics and incorporate the tech atmosphere in the theme. Using computer chips
    and robots as thematic elements brought about consistent branding that is easily
    applicable across different media.

    Birth of Oso (The Bear)

    One of the main goals is to market the conference innovatively using unique visual
    representation. At first, I came across the idea of a bear character as just a design
    element on a FB event cover I had made. Erin, FASTER’s founder and event organizer,
    liked the bear a lot that she wanted to put them on all the marketing designs.
    Eventually, the “FASTER Bear” became almost like a logo for FASTERCon (I had
    secretly named the bear Oso -- Tagalog word for “bear”). I had the most fun making
    this character, and seeing Oso printed on stickers and keychains made me proud.

    Brand Expansion

    Recently, FASTER has undergone major changes. The organization expanded to
    include not just Silicon Valley tech pros, but all those interested in STE(A)M across
    the nation (now branching out to New York). With this change, I extended the design
    to be more inclusive of other fields.

    Increasing Visibility

    FASTERCon needed designs for social media marketing. The goal was to create a
    banner that attracts attention as people scroll through their feeds. By using bright
    colors and highlighting the FASTER Bear, the banner was difficult to ignore.

    For the day of the event, a simple physical banner was designed with the same
    theme as the digital. The banner was posted outside the venue as a signpost.

    Program Logistics & Highlights

    The event included speaker sessions, Q&A panel, workshops and networking
    activities. For this, I designed a 12-page printed pamphlet to inform attendees about
    event logistics (schedule, speakers, sponsors, etc.). If attendees missed
    announcements or arrived in the middle of the day, they will always know what is
    going on, where to go, and who are on stage.

    Obstacles

    The work was done remotely, and sometimes communication via emails do not get
    ideas across as smoothly as face-to-face discussions. There could have been clearer
    conversations when specifying requirements, especially for the pamphlet. Sometimes
    I received sponsor logos that had low resolution, or bios that were either too short or
    too long. The experience has taught me to be better at planning ahead by designing
    flexibly for these situations.

    Learnings

    I learned how to adjust my designs according to the client’s wants and needs. As a
    student, I usually have creative freedom when designing for student organizations or
    for personal pursuits. Working with FASTER was my first time collaborating with
    industry professionals, and I learned that there are limitations when working with
    people who have different ideas. Ultimately, I learned the importance of designing for
    others.
